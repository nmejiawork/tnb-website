AI governance is the set of policies, processes, and oversight mechanisms that determine how an organization builds, deploys, and monitors AI. An AI governance lead owns that function, which includes defining acceptable use policies, running risk assessments on AI projects, ensuring compliance with regulations like the EU AI Act, and maintaining model documentation (model cards) that audit-ready teams need.

The role sits at the intersection of legal, technical, and organizational concerns. A governance lead needs to understand what models can and cannot do, where they can fail, how to test for those failures, and how to explain all of this to boards, regulators, and customers. They work closely with the CAIO where one exists, and with legal, security, and data teams.

For most builders at early-stage companies, AI governance is not yet a dedicated role. The function lives informally inside engineering, product, or legal. But as organizations scale their AI usage, as more AI decisions affect real users, and as regulatory obligations arrive with hard deadlines, governance becomes a genuine staffing need. Teams building AI products that touch regulated industries, healthcare, finance, or employment, are already feeling this pressure.
